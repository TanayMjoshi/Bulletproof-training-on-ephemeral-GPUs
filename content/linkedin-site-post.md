## Post body

```
Most engineering write-ups ask you to just trust the author. I wanted mine to be something you could break yourself. 🧪

So I turned my piece on resumable ML training into an interactive page.

You start a training run, then kill it mid-way (like a real GPU disconnect) and watch what happens: without the pattern it drops back to zero and loses everything, with the pattern it resumes from the last checkpoint and loses nothing.

Five hands-on demos, one per idea:
• checkpoint the full state
• atomic writes (try to corrupt a save)
• a "done marker" for sweeps
• durable vs ephemeral storage (recycle the machine, see what survives)
• resume vs restart (watch the learning-rate curve)

No signup, nothing to install. Just click and break things.

Link in the comments 👇
```
