# Titles, hashtags & posting plan

## Title options (pick per platform)

1. **Never lose a training run again: a checkpoint-and-resume playbook for ephemeral GPUs**
   *(Best for Dev.to/Medium — descriptive, keyword-rich for search.)*
2. **A disconnect should cost seconds, not hours: resumable training on free GPUs**
   *(Strong outcome-first hook. Great for Medium and the LinkedIn article.)*
3. **I stopped losing training runs to GPU disconnects. Here's the exact pattern.**
   *(First-person, high curiosity — best for LinkedIn.)*
4. **Bulletproof training on ephemeral GPUs: checkpoint, resume, never restart**
   *(Punchy, "bulletproof" framing — good fit for Peerlist / X.)*
5. **Make your training loop idempotent and the Colab disconnect stops mattering**
   *(Concept-forward; appeals to the engineering-minded crowd.)*

**Recommended mapping**
- Dev.to / Medium → Title 1 or 2
- LinkedIn article → Title 3
- Peerlist → Title 4
- X thread → no formal title; the hook in tweet 1 does the work

## Hashtags by platform

`#MachineLearning #MLEngineering #DeepLearning #PyTorch #MLOps`

**X / Twitter** (1–2 max in the *final* tweet only — hashtags mid-thread hurt reach)
`#MachineLearning #PyTorch`

**Dev.to** (max 4, lowercase, no spaces)
`machinelearning, python, pytorch, mlops`

**Medium** (up to 5)
`Machine Learning, MLOps, PyTorch, Deep Learning, Programming`

**Peerlist** (3 focused tags)
`Machine Learning, MLOps, PyTorch`

## Best day/time to post (US/Europe-leaning dev audience)

All times are **your local time**; shift to your audience's timezone if it differs.

- **LinkedIn:** Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 a.m. (commute/first-coffee scroll) or ~12 p.m. Avoid Fri afternoon → Sun.
- **X/Twitter:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 a.m. or a second window ~5–6 p.m. Tech threads also do well Sunday evening.
- **Dev.to:** Tuesday or Wednesday morning (US ET). Dev.to has a long tail via tags/search, so timing matters less than tags + cover image.
- **Medium:** Tuesday–Thursday morning; Medium's distribution is slow-burn, so publish then keep sharing the link.
- **Peerlist:** Weekday mornings; the launch/Spotlight feed favors fresh weekday posts.

## Recommended ordering & cadence

Publish the **canonical long-form first**, let it get indexed, then point everything at it.

**Day 1 (Tue), morning**
1. Publish on **Dev.to** (set as canonical). Attach the diagram as cover image.
2. Publish on **Medium** with `canonical_url` pointing to the Dev.to post (avoids SEO self-competition).
3. Post the **LinkedIn article** (it's native long-form — fine to live in parallel; it builds your LinkedIn presence directly).

**Day 1, ~1–2 hours later**
4. Post the **LinkedIn feed post** with the diagram, link to the article **in the first comment** (LinkedIn suppresses posts with outbound links in the body — put the link in comment #1 and mention "link in comments").

**Day 2 (Wed), morning**
5. Post the **X thread**. Put the link only in the last tweet. Pin the thread to your profile for a week.

**Day 3–4**
6. Post on **Peerlist** linking to the Medium or Dev.to version.

**Day 7**
7. Re-share the LinkedIn feed post / re-up the X thread with a different one-line hook ("One week ago I posted this; the #1 question was about X…"). Recycling a proven post is normal and effective.

## Two quick distribution tips
- On LinkedIn and X, reply to your own first comment within the first hour — early engagement compounds reach.
- Cross-link: once the canonical is live, add "Originally posted on [Dev.to/Medium]" footers everywhere so all versions reinforce one URL's authority.
